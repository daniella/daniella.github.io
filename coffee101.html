DIFFERENT TYPES OF COFFEE DRINKS

With so many coffee drink options these days, it can often get confusing to know the difference between one drink and another. In some drinks, even the amount of milk foam added has come down to a science.

Above is an infographic of nine of the most popular coffee drinks and what ingredients are needed to make them courtesy of coffeeinvestigator.com.

FACTS ON SOME OF THE MOST POPULAR COFFEE DRINKS:

Courtesy of The Huffington Post

Espresso: Usually has about 30 ml of caffeine

Espresso Macchiato: Contains a shot of espresso (or sometimes two) and a dot of foamed milk.

Caffé Latte: The latte gets its name because it's full of milk. It is generally just a 60 ml shot of espresso with 300 ml steamed milk -- and only 2ml foamed milk.

Cappuccino: One of the most popular espresso drinks! A cappucino done right should have equal parts of espresso, steamed milk and foamed milk (about 60 ml each).

Caffé Mocha: With 60 ml of espresso, 60 ml of chocolate and 30 ml of steamed milk.

Americano: Contains a 30-ml shot of espresso and twice the amount of water of an espresso (60 ml).
